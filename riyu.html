Early Life

Leo Tolstoy was born on September 9, 1828, in Yasnaya Polyana, Russia. He was the fourth of five children in a noble family. His early life was marked by tragedy, as he lost his parents at a young age, leading him to be raised by relatives.

Major Works

Tolstoy is best known for his epic novels War and Peace (1869) and Anna Karenina (1877). These masterpieces delve deeply into themes of love, family, and society, and have secured his place as one of the greatest authors of all time.

Philosophy and Later Life

In his later years, Tolstoy embraced a Christian anarchist philosophy, advocating for nonviolence, simplicity, and self-sufficiency. His ideas influenced figures like Mahatma Gandhi and Martin Luther King Jr.
